About Kevin Smith

Kevin Patrick Smith was born in Red Bank, New Jersey, to Grace (Schultz) and Donald E. Smith, a postal worker.

His first movie, Clerks (1994), was filmed in the convenience store in which Smith worked. He was only allowed to shoot at night after the store closed. This movie won the highest award at the Sundance film festival and was brought to theaters by Miramax.

Since Clerks, Kevin has gone on to direct multiple motion pictures and garnering a large cult fanbase. Today, he makes movies that he wants to make, and runs the Smodco podcast network, where he stars in podcasts such as Fatman on Batman and Hollywood Babble-On.
